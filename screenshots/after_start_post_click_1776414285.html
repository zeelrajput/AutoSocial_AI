CEO @ Woodpecker, +9 years in cold email

Promoted by Woodpecker.co

Josh Braun got blacklisted for one cold email. That mistake taught him the most important lesson in outbound. 👇🏻 It’s not about what you send. It’s about how you make people feel. He sent the same message to 12 people at one company. Each one forwarded it to the decision-maker. The reply? “Josh, anyone who uses this approach ends up on my blacklist.” Because spam doesn’t scale. Trust does. Since then, he’s taught a better approach: ☑ Surprise your reader ☑ Prove you paid attention ☑ Offer something they care about ☑ Don’t just personalize - be personal People don’t remember pitches. They remember how you made them feel - respected, understood, seen. That’s what turns cold emails into conversations, and conversations into deals. What’s one cold email you still remember? (For the right or wrong reasons) PS Always limit how many people you contact from the same company. I’d cap it at 3 per week - max.

Hiring Fresher's (Only for Indians)

Work From Home

Hiring freshers can bring fresh energy, new perspectives, and a long-term investment in talent.

While they may lack direct experience, fresh graduates often come with a strong academic foundation, a willingness to learn, and a high level of adaptability.

They are eager to prove themselves and can quickly adapt to company culture, bringing innovative ideas and enthusiasm to the workplace.

By hiring freshers, companies can mold and shape talent to meet their specific needs, building a workforce that is loyal and aligned with the company's values and goals.

Additionally, freshers are often more cost-effective in terms of salary and can grow within the organization, reducing recruitment costs in the long run. Investing in young talent also helps create a diverse and dynamic team, ensuring that businesses stay ahead of industry trends by fostering a culture of learning and continuous improvement.
